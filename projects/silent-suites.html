Silent Suites

A modular office phone booth that drops interior noise by 32 dB while shipping flat.

Role
Lead Design Engineer
Client
—
Year
2025

The brief

Open-plan offices needed a quiet space for calls, but existing booths were heavy, expensive to ship, and took a day to install. The brief: a one-person booth that meets a 30 dB sound-reduction target, ships as flat panels through a standard doorway, and assembles in under 30 minutes with no specialist tools — all under a €1,800 unit cost.

Key decisions

Two calls shaped the whole design. First, a decoupled double-wall with a 40 mm air gap and a mass-loaded vinyl core — chosen over thicker foam because the mass-spring-mass system kills the 200–2,000 Hz speech band where it matters, at half the weight. Second, a cam-lock panel joint instead of screws: FEA showed the corner posts carried the load, so the panels could be non-structural and snap together, which is what made the flat-pack and 30-minute assembly possible.

Outcome

The production booth hit 32 dB of reduction — two over target — and ships as six flat panels that pass through an 80 cm door.
